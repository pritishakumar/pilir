Our Vision & Mission:

At Pilir we believe that the wellbeing of children is a major part of what can drive the world forward in a better way. To do this requires enabling parents to have as much energy and time as possible to meet their needs and do their work to provide. and enabling caregivers

We seek to provide quality childcare through programs that not only keep children safe and occupied, but enrich their lives. In addition, we'd like to make it easier for parents and childcare providers to connect. To optimize the balance between costs to parents, and fair renumeration for childcare providers, which will draw the best talent.

Lastly, we will work to bring awareness about policies and drive policy change that will keep our children from harm - physical, emotional, or psychological. We seek to provide tools to parents and caregivers that will enable this...

Pilir Leadership

Danica Marsden is from Vancouver, BC Canada. She has always loved working with children, as a camp counselor, ski instructor, tutor and mentor. She earned her doctorate in physics at the University of Pennsylvania and while there, worked with the homeless at Philly ReStart and served on the UPenn academic disciplinary hearing board. While working in Calgary, Danica was a mentor with the Schulich robotics program, where children ages 5-14 learned how to build robots. She moved back to the Vancouver area to assist some family members, and during that time heard about the Squamish townhall meetings on the childcare crisis, which inspired her to look for a way to help solve this problem.
